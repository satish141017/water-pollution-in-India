National River Conservation Plan (NRCP): The government launched NRCP in 1995 to clean up major rivers in India. Under this plan, the government has identified polluted stretches of 34 rivers across the country, and several projects have been implemented to reduce pollution levels.
National Water Mission : Launched in 2011, the National Water Mission aims to conserve water resources and reduce pollution in water bodies across the country. The mission also focuses on promoting sustainable water management practices.
Swachh Bharat Abhiyan: Launched in 2014, this campaign aims to make India clean and free of open defecation. The campaign also focuses on solid waste management, which is a major contributor to water pollution.
Clean Ganga Mission: Launched in 2015, the mission aims to clean up the Ganga river, which is one of the most polluted rivers in the world. The government has allocated significant funds for this mission, and several projects are underway to reduce pollution levels in the river.
