Water Pollution
Situation in India

India faces a severe water pollution crisis, with a majority of its water bodies being contaminated by industrial, agricultural, and domestic waste. According to a report by the Central Pollution Control Board (CPCB), around 80% of India's surface water is polluted. Inadequate treatment of sewage is a significant contributor to water pollution in India, with over 60% of India's sewage being discharged untreated into rivers and other water bodies. The pollution of India's rivers, including the Ganges, Yamuna, and Godavari, is a major concern. These rivers are not only significant sources of water for millions of people but also hold great cultural and religious importance. However, they have become severely polluted, and the contamination has led to severe health hazards and environmental degradation.
